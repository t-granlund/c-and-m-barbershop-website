Find where your domain lives ~10 min

Your web address, candmbarbershop.net, is registered with a company that charges you once a year. Before anything else, you need to know which company. That company is called your registrar.

How to find it

  1. Open your email (the one you use for business receipts).
  2. Search for candmbarbershop.net or the phrase domain renewal.
  3. Look at who sent the renewal email. That's your registrar.

It's almost certainly one of these five

  • Squarespace — if you ever used "Google Domains," you're here now. Google Domains shut down in 2023 and every account moved to Squarespace.
  • GoDaddy — green logo, loud ads.
  • Namecheap — orange logo.
  • Cloudflare — orange cloud logo.
  • Porkbun — yes, that's a real one.
⚠️ Don't guess on this step.

If you can't find the email after 15 minutes of looking, text a tech-savvy friend or family member and ask them to help you locate it. This is the #1 step where people get stuck. Get it right before moving on.

Also: your current Google Sites page

If your current website is the default Google Sites template, the custom-domain mapping in Google Sites needs to be removed before the new site can go live. If you choose "Pay a Pro" in Step 2, they'll handle this for you. If you go DIY, Cloudflare will walk you through it.

Put the site online ~30 min

🟢 Path A — Pay a Pro

Post a small job on Fiverr or Upwork. Anyone qualified can do this in under an hour.

  • Zip up the whole website folder (right-click → Compress / Send to → Compressed folder).
  • Go to Fiverr or Upwork and post a job.
  • Send them the message below, and share your registrar login only after you've hired them (never in the job posting itself).
  • Budget $30–75. Expect a 24-hour turnaround.
Hi! I have a static website in a zip file. Please deploy it to Cloudflare Pages (free tier) and connect it to my domain candmbarbershop.net. If there's an existing Google Sites mapping, please remove it first. I'll share my domain-registrar login securely after we agree on the job. Thank you!
Find help on Fiverr → Try Upwork →

When they're done, skip ahead to Step 5: Phone-test the live site. You're basically finished.

🟡 Path B — Do it yourself (free, ~30 min)

You'll use Cloudflare Pages. It's free, has no traffic limits, and is made by Cloudflare — a large, reliable company.

  • Go to dash.cloudflare.com/sign-up and create a free account. Use your business email. Save the password somewhere safe.
  • Check your email and click the verification link they send you.
  • In the Cloudflare dashboard, click Workers & Pages in the left sidebar.
  • Click Create → Pages → Upload assets.
  • Name your project cm-barber-shop. Click Create project.
  • Drag your website folder onto the upload box. Wait about 30 seconds for it to finish.
  • Your site is live at something like cm-barber-shop.pages.dev. Copy that address and text it to yourself — you'll want it handy.
Start here: Create a Cloudflare account →
🎉 Celebrate!

That .pages.dev address works right now on any phone or computer. Text it to a friend and make sure everything looks good before you point your real web address at it.

4

Connect your real web address ~15 min + waiting

DIY only — if you paid a pro, they already did this.

Right now your site lives at a free .pages.dev address. This step points candmbarbershop.net at it.

  • Back in your Cloudflare Pages project, click the Custom domains tab.
  • Click Set up a custom domain → type candmbarbershop.net → Continue.
  • Repeat to also add www.candmbarbershop.net.
  • Cloudflare will show you one of two things: a CNAME record or a pair of nameservers. Write down whatever it shows you — you'll paste this at your registrar next.
  • Open a new tab and log into your registrar (from Step 1). Find the DNS or Nameservers section.
  • Paste in what Cloudflare told you. Save.
  • Wait 5–60 minutes. Your real web address will start working on its own.
⚠️ Padlock icon may be missing at first

For the first hour or so, your browser might say "not secure" or show a certificate warning. That's normal — the padlock takes a little time to appear. Wait 30 minutes and check again. It always arrives.

🚨 Don't panic if it seems broken

Propagation (the fancy word for "the internet learning your new address") can take up to an hour. If it's not working after 2 hours, clear your browser cache or try on a different phone. Still stuck? Go to Step 8.

6

Turn on analytics ~5 min · optional

Want to know how many people visit your site each week? Cloudflare gives you a free, private analytics dashboard. No cookies, no pop-up banners, no selling data.

  • Log in at dash.cloudflare.com.
  • Click Analytics & Logs → Web Analytics.
  • Click Add a site → enter candmbarbershop.net.
  • Cloudflare gives you a token — a long string of letters and numbers. Copy it.
  • Open index.html in VS Code (see Step 7). Press Ctrl+F (Windows) or ⌘+F (Mac) and search for REPLACE_WITH_TOKEN. Replace it with the token. Save.
  • Re-upload the folder to Cloudflare Pages (drag-and-drop again, same as Step 3).
💡 You can skip this step and come back later.

The website works fine either way. Analytics just let you see traffic counts and which pages people visit. Until you paste the token, the code is harmless and does nothing.

How to edit things later

🚨 Critical — use the right editor

Download VS Code first: code.visualstudio.com. It's free, made by Microsoft, and doesn't corrupt files.

Do NOT use TextEdit (Mac) or regular Notepad (Windows). They secretly change the file format and will break the website. This is the #1 cause of broken small-business sites.

On Windows, Notepad++ is also safe and free.

After any edit, you have to re-upload the whole folder to Cloudflare Pages (drag-and-drop, same as Step 3). Cloudflare keeps the old version until the new upload finishes, so you can't break the live site mid-upload.

📞 A stylist's phone number changed
  1. Open index.html in VS Code.
  2. Press Ctrl+F / ⌘+F and search for the old number.
  3. Change it in every place it appears — the visible text, the tel: link, and the sms: link. Usually 3 places per stylist.
  4. Save. Re-upload the folder to Cloudflare Pages.

Feels risky? Text Tyler a screenshot of the old + new number. 10-minute fix for someone who knows HTML.

📅 Your hours changed
  1. In index.html, search for Shop Hours and update the visible times.
  2. Also update the HOURS table near the top of script.js — this is what powers the live "Open now" dot.
  3. Save both files. Re-upload.

If you only update index.html and forget script.js, the "Open now" dot will be wrong. When in doubt, text Tyler.

👋 A stylist left, or a new one joined

Honestly: pay a pro $20 on Fiverr or text Tyler a photo of the business card. This is the one edit where the copy-paste is finicky enough that hiring help saves frustration.

If you're determined to do it yourself: find an existing stylist's card block in index.html (search for a name you know), copy the whole <article>...</article> block, paste it next to the original, and change the name, role, initials, and phone number. Save. Re-upload.

📸 Adding a photo
  1. Put the photo file (JPG or PNG) in the same folder as the other files. Keep it under 500 KB so the site loads fast on phones — use the free tool at squoosh.app to shrink it.
  2. In index.html, add: <img src="your-photo.jpg" alt="Describe the photo in words"> wherever you want it to appear.
  3. Save. Re-upload.

Top-priority photos to eventually add (per your brand directives):

  • A warm, brightly-lit shot of the shop interior.
  • One photo featuring a woman or child in the chair (makes it crystal-clear everyone's welcome).
  • Individual headshots of each stylist.

If something goes wrong

Take a breath. Your files are backed up in more than one place. The live site can always be restored.

In order, try these

  1. Text Tyler. He built this, he can usually fix it in 10 minutes.
  2. Post a Fiverr job. Title it "Fix my Cloudflare Pages site." Budget $30–75. Usually fixed within 24 hours. Attach your website folder.
  3. Restore from backup. Your 6 files are safe in at least 3 places: this folder, your email, and the GitHub repo Tyler set up. Any web developer can redeploy the site in 30 minutes from any of those copies.

Common problems & quick fixes

The site is showing the wrong information

The Cloudflare Pages dashboard keeps every version you've ever uploaded. Go to your project → Deployments tab → find the last known good version → click the three-dot menu → Rollback to this deployment. You're back to the old version in 30 seconds.

"Certificate expired" or "not secure" warning

Cloudflare renews certificates automatically every 90 days. If you ever see an actual expired warning, it almost always means the custom domain setup from Step 4 got disconnected. Remove the custom domain in the Cloudflare Pages dashboard and re-add it — this triggers a fresh certificate within 15 minutes.

The domain expired / stopped working

Log into your registrar (Step 1) and renew. If you're within 30 days of expiry, a regular renewal works. After 30 days, there's a redemption fee (~$80) and you have to act fast before it's released to the public.

Prevention: turn on auto-renew at your registrar. This is the single most important "forget it" step.

Your Cloudflare account got locked or deleted
  1. Create a new free Cloudflare account.
  2. Drag your website folder onto Cloudflare Pages Direct Upload.
  3. Re-add the custom domain (Step 4).
  4. At your registrar, update the DNS/nameservers to the new Cloudflare settings.

Total time: ~30 minutes. No files are lost.

The credential list (write this on paper, keep in the shop's file cabinet)

  • Domain registrar name + email + password
  • Cloudflare account email + password
  • A copy of the website folder on a USB drive, labeled cm-website-backup with today's date
  • A zip of the website folder emailed to yourself
  • Auto-renew turned on at your registrar
🚨 Don't store passwords in a shared Google Doc

Write them on paper. Put the paper in a locked filing cabinet at the shop. Only people you trust should know where. This is more secure than almost any digital option for a business your size.
